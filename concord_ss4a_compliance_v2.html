🏅
Lane Reduction Award Consideration — Cowell Road Corridor (COR-01)
The SS4A FY26 NOFO includes an Award Consideration for applications that include lane reduction as a core safety treatment. Cowell Rd Corridor (COR-01) proposes raised median channelization that physically reduces the number of through travel lanes available to vehicles, directing traffic through structured turning movements. This meets the lane reduction definition per SS4A guidance. Recommendation: Clearly label this as "Lane Reduction" in the project narrative and budget description.
📡
Demonstration Activity — C-V2X Connected Vehicle Infrastructure (CW-08)
Deployment of 21 C-V2X RSUs at every documented fatality location in Concord qualifies as an SS4A Demonstration Activity under the "innovative technology deployment at scale" category. The USDOT Intersection Safety Challenge Tier 1 win (DERQ, 2025) directly supports federal recognition of this technology. Recommendation: Position the C-V2X + DERQ deployment as a replicable model for mid-size California cities and quantify the % of fatal locations covered (100%).
🌟
Equity Community Award Eligibility — Monument Blvd Corridor
Monument Blvd intersections (INT-03, INT-09, INT-11, INT-20) and the Lacey Ln location serve census tracts with documented equity concerns. If confirmed as Disadvantaged Community tracts per DOT's Equity Framework, this strengthens the application's equity narrative and may provide preference consideration. Recommendation: Verify APP (Advancing People Programs) census tract designation for Monument Blvd corridor and explicitly document in the equity section.
🔬
Before/After Evaluation — Program Sustainability Narrative
The SUPP scope includes RSA, CPTED, C-V2X evaluation, CSAP update cycle, and before/after data collection. This positions Concord as a learning agency committed to measuring effectiveness — a valued quality in SS4A applications. Recommendation: In the application narrative, commit to specific evaluation metrics: % KSI reduction targets at each HIN site, timeline for interim data reporting, and TIMS query methodology for before/after comparison.
⚠️
Evaluator Perspective — Items That Could Raise Questions or Be Strengthened: The following gaps were identified by reviewing this application through the lens of an SS4A federal evaluator. Addressing these before submission will strengthen competitiveness.
🔴High Priority — Address Before Submission3 Items
!
Equity Census Tract Documentation: Monument Blvd equity community designation should be explicitly documented with census tract numbers, DAC status, and population data. Evaluators look for this to award equity credit.
!
Quantified KSI Reduction Targets: While CMFs are cited, the application should include projected KSI reductions per project or program area (e.g., "LPI at 18 intersections projected to reduce ped KSI by X% based on CMF 0.71"). Evaluators expect quantified impact projections.
!
Local Match Funding Documentation: The $2.32M local match must be documented with fund source, account numbers, and authorization. "Measure V" and "CIP" should be linked to specific appropriation authority. Evaluators flag unsecured match as a risk.
🟡Medium Priority — Strengthen if Time Allows4 Items
~
Engagement Documentation Depth: 263 survey responses are good but evaluators want to see HOW communities were reached — language access, meeting dates, how feedback changed project selection. A 1-page engagement summary exhibit strengthens this.
~
INT-16 "4 Remaining HIN" Bundling: Bundling 4 intersections (Commerce/Concord, Bailey/Clayton, Monument/Victory, Bates/Commercial) into one line item without individual budgets may draw evaluator questions. Recommend providing per-intersection breakdowns.
~
C-V2X Operational Readiness: Evaluators will ask: who operates the RSUs after deployment? What is the data governance plan? Is Concord's TMC staffed to receive V2I data? A one-paragraph operations plan mitigates this concern.
~
Speed Feedback Sign Unit Costing: 75 signs at CW-01 — evaluators may question unit cost consistency. Provide a unit cost breakdown (sign + installation + maintenance) to preempt cost reasonableness questions.
🟢Low Risk — Minor Refinements3 Items
✓
ITERIS vs. DERQ Overlap: Narrative should clarify that ITERIS provides adaptive signal control while DERQ INSIGHT provides AI near-miss analytics — they are complementary, not duplicative. Evaluators unfamiliar with California signal systems may question both vendors.
✓
Maintenance Commitments: For all new physical infrastructure (bulb-outs, PPLS lighting, HAWKs), document the maintenance program and funding source. SS4A evaluators look for long-term sustainability.
✓
Behavioral Program Metrics: CW-10 Safe Routes education program should include projected participation numbers, delivery partners (schools, community orgs), and measurable outcomes beyond "awareness."
💡Enhancement Opportunities — Competitive Edge3 Items
+
CHP / OTS Partnership Letters: A letter from CHP confirming collision data quality and from OTS confirming coordination significantly boosts evaluator confidence in data integrity and implementation capacity.
+
Replicability Statement: For the C-V2X / DERQ demonstration, explicitly state how this model will be shared with other California cities through MTC, Caltrans, or CSAC. Replicability is a key SS4A Demonstration Activity criterion.
+
Speed Safety Camera Program Reference: If California SB 1 / speed camera pilot legislation advances, reference Concord's readiness to integrate speed camera data into the DERQ/TMC platform as a future phase. Signals forward-thinking safety culture.
